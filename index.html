IN LOVING MEMORY

Eric Masika Barasa

1992 - 2016

HIS LIFE

Birth and life

Eric was born on 7th December 1992 at Aga Khan Hospital. He is the second born of Augustus and MaryStella Barasa. He comes after Loius Barasa and his other brother is Bill Barasa. Eric was a Christian, was baptized at St Elizabeth ACK Church Munami and went to the youth service at All Saints Cathedral.

Eric Started School in New Light Academy in Nairobi. When the family moved to Mombasa, Eric went to Aga Khan Primary Mombasa. After two years, Eric went to St Peters Primary Boarding school. When the brother finished stand eight Eric requested to move back home, and he schooled at Kingsway Academy in Malindi where he sat his class 8. In 2008, he joined Pioneer School in Maragua and completed his forth form in 2011. Eric later joined Technical University of Kenya in 2012 to pursue Bachelor of Science degree in ‘Urban Design’ which he was in his last semester.

Hobbies & Community Work

Eric loved playing football. He played full back three. He was a crucial member of his high school team that reached provincial semi-finals as was formerly known in 2010. He also played for his Technical University of Kenya team. He was a Chelsea fun and called himself ‘Ashley Cole’. He loved singing, poetry, dancing and visiting children’s homes. He was part of the ‘Open Mike group’ at his campus. Eric was a member of the Open Mic team– An arts and Music group. The group is involved in singing, poetry, dancing, sharing life experiences and positive ideas and visiting children’s homes. The team mobilized resources and visited the street and orphaned children. Eric took leadership resource mobilization, cooking and playing with the children.

Illness

Eric was diagnosed with brain cancer on 23rd April 2015 at Nairobi Hospital. On May 06, 2015, Eric underwent an eight hour brain surgery at Christian Medical College hospital in Tamil Nadu, India. The surgeons removed most of the tumor from his brain. He underwent radiation and chemotherapy treatment and subsequently he underwent another six months of chemotherapy. On May 16th 2016, he was diagnosed with another tumor growing towards the spine which was affecting his speech, memory and nervous system. On 3rd June 2016, he traveled to India to seek further treatment and returned to Kenya on 23rd June 2016. On returning home, his condition changed drastically for the worst. He was hospitalized and on 2nd July 2016 he breathed his last. May God the Almighty rest his soul in eternal peace. Eric we loved you, but God love more.
